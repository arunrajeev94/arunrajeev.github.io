Lo-fi Prototyping

Lo-fi prototyping done as part of Design Development for an app that uses Augmented Reality to teach embroidery. The excercise was undertaken as part of a group assignment from the CS6021 module, Foundations of Interactive Media Design that explored User centric approaches to Design and the various concepts of a User Centered Approach to Design.

Lo-fi paper protoypes were developed for screen based and Augmented Reality interactions as part of the assignment and usability tests were conducted to further iterate and improve the Designs. The physical prototypes helped in leraning about the pros and cons of the proposed Design Solution and paved way for further iterations based on user testing.It was learnt during user testing that Lo-fi prototypes were key in conveying the concept to a majority of users.

Arduino synthesizer

The Arduino Synthesizer or Teensy Synthesizer is the final project undertaken as part of the CS6351, Physical Computing Module. The project is a culmination of the first semester's learning and exploration about the basics of electronics and code using the Arduino Uno platform.

This was a group assignment and the goal was to come up with a final project based on an Arduino platform. The concept of hauntology, as propogated by Mark Fisher as a theme was explored and was manifested in the form of a synthesizer based on the Teensy 3.2 module. The Teensy 3.2 module was used due to the presence of an in-built DAC that helped get a direct output to a set of speakers or headphones. The Teensy board also had a good purpose built library in the Arduino IDE for Audio synthesis and was utilized for this project.

Photography

These set of photographs were taken as part of an assignment for the CS6041 module. The photographs have been captured using a Canon EOS 3000D using stock 18-55mm EF-S Lens.

The photographs are based on the theme of life and have been captured in and around the University of Limerick campus. They were shot as RAW images and have been post processed in Adobe Lightroom. Each photograph had a basic exposure correction, Lens profile and chromatic abberation correction and basic color correction.
